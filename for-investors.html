For investors & long-term residents

Long-term residency, without locking up capital.

Most routes to a long stay abroad ask you to buy something — property, bonds, a stake in a business — and keep it. The Thailand Privilege Visa takes a different shape: a one-time membership that secures 5 to 20 years of multi-entry residency, with no qualifying investment and nothing tied up.

Be clear-eyed

What it is — and what it isn't.

It is easy to file this under "golden visa" and assume the usual mechanics. It works differently, and the differences cut both ways, so they are worth stating plainly.

  • It is a membership, not an investment. No property purchase, no bond holding, no business funding is required to qualify.
  • It is residency, not employment. The visa lets you live in Thailand; it does not grant the right to work here.
  • It is fixed-term, not permanent. It provides 5 to 20 years of residency and is not a path to permanent residence or citizenship.
  • It is predictable. The cost is a known, one-time fee — not capital exposed to a market or a developer.

If a path to permanent residence or a return on capital is the goal, this may not be your route — and we'll say so. See eligibility and approval for the full picture.

Why it appeals

The case for capital left free.

  • No capital tied up. There is no deposit to maintain and no asset to hold, so your capital stays where you choose to put it.
  • No income or net-worth test. Eligibility turns on a clean security background, not on proving wealth — the requirement is the same for everyone.
  • A known, one-time cost. You pay a single membership fee for the whole term; there are no annual membership fees to renew.
  • A long, stable footing. Tiers run up to twenty years, giving a dependable base for time spent in the region.
Choosing a tier

Which tier matches the horizon?

For a long-term resident, the tier is mostly a question of term length and whether family is joining.

  • A measured start — Bronze or Gold. Five years to establish a footing, with the simplest entry at Bronze, whose application window runs to 30 September 2026.
  • A serious horizon — Diamond. Fifteen years, with a substantial annual points allocation for those spending real time in Thailand.
  • The longest footing — Reserve. Twenty years, by invitation, the most committed tier and the deepest benefits.
  • Bringing family — Platinum and above. Family add-ons begin at Platinum, so households planning together start there.

Compare all five and their terms on the programs page, or use the tier finder.

On cost and tax

In short

Investor questions, answered plainly.

  • Is this an investment visa? No. There is no required investment — it's a one-time membership for long-stay residency.
  • Do I have to buy property or bonds? No. Nothing is purchased or locked up to qualify.
  • Will it lead to permanent residence or citizenship? No. It's fixed-term residency, not a path to PR or citizenship.
