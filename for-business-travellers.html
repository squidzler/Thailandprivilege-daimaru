Make Thailand your base for all of Asia.

Bangkok sits a few hours' flight from almost everywhere in the region, and the Thailand Privilege Visa (formerly Thailand Elite Visa) lets you treat it as home between trips. Why base yourself here

A fixed address in a moving region.

People who live out of a carry-on tend to reach the same conclusion: it is far easier to range across Asia from a single, settled base than to keep restarting somewhere new. Thailand is a natural choice for that base, and the Privilege Visa removes the immigration friction that usually comes with staying put as a foreigner.

  • Multi-entry, by design. Come and go as often as your schedule demands — every return is on the same membership, with no fresh visa to arrange.
  • No visa runs, no border bounces. The recurring scramble of tourist-entry stays simply ends; your residency is settled for the full term of your tier.
  • A regional hub on your doorstep. Suvarnabhumi and Don Mueang put much of East and Southeast Asia within easy reach for work or a long weekend.
  • Set up once. A lease, a bank relationship, a routine — worth building when you know the visa underneath it lasts 5 to 20 years.

It is a long-stay residency visa, not a work permit, and not a path to permanent residence or citizenship. See full eligibility and approval details.

Choosing a tier

Which tier matches your mileage?

For a travelling base, two things decide the tier: how long you expect to keep Thailand as home, and how much you value the travel perks that points unlock.

  • A short commitment — Bronze. Five years, the simplest entry, with airport fast track and all core services. The most-applied tier; its application window runs to 30 September 2026.
  • Travel perks that matter — Gold and up. Annual Privilege Points begin at Gold and fund the limousine and lounge benefits frequent flyers actually use.
  • A long regional horizon — Diamond. Fifteen years for one person settling Thailand in as a lasting base of operations.
  • The longest base — Reserve. Twenty years, by invitation, with the deepest points allocation for those who travel constantly.

Not sure which? The tier finder suggests one in about a minute, or compare them all on the programs page.

An honest line

A residence, not a work permit.

It matters to be precise here, because the distinction trips people up. The Thailand Privilege Visa gives you the right to live in Thailand — not the right to work in it.

  • Taking local employment with a Thai company requires a separate work permit; the membership does not provide one.
  • Using Thailand as a base while your work or business sits abroad or online is a common pattern among members — but how that is treated for tax and immigration depends on your own situation.
  • We don't give tax or legal advice. For anything touching where and how you earn, speak with a qualified professional before you commit.

A spouse and children can join from the Platinum tier upward — useful if family travels with you. Family rules are on the programs page.

On cost

One fee, then years of use.

  • A one-time membership fee. From ฿650,000 (~$19,000 USD) for Bronze, covering the entire term — there are no annual membership fees to renew.
  • Nothing upfront. You pay only after your application is approved, directly to Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd. — never to Daimaru.
  • Cost per year of base falls the longer you stay. Spread across a 10-, 15-, or 20-year tier, the membership reads very differently from a fee paid for five.
In short

Traveller questions, answered plainly.

  • How often can I leave and re-enter? As often as you like. The visa is multi-entry for its full term, so there is no cap on trips and no new visa between them.
  • Will I still do visa runs? No. The fixed-term membership replaces the tourist-entry cycle entirely.
  • Can I work remotely from Thailand? The visa grants residency, not the right to work locally. Remote work for an overseas employer is a personal tax and immigration question — take advice.
  • Does it cover travel insurance? No. The membership is a visa and concierge service, not insurance — arrange travel and health cover separately.
