01 — General

Before you apply.

What is the Thailand Privilege Visa?

The Thailand Privilege Visa — formerly known as Thailand Elite — is a long-term, multi-entry residency program operated by Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd., a Thai state enterprise. A single membership grants between 5 and 20 years of residency, along with airport fast track, a personal liaison, and a 24/7 member contact center.

Who is eligible?

The program is open to foreign nationals with a valid passport who pass Thai Immigration's background check. There is no minimum income or investment requirement to apply — approval is based on the security screening. We'll confirm your eligibility before you commit to anything.

How much does it cost?

Membership fees range from ฿650,000 for the 5-year Bronze tier up to ฿5,000,000 for the 20-year Reserve tier. See the full comparison for every tier.

The fee is paid directly to Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd. Daimaru does not charge a separate agent fee.

When do I pay?

Only after your application is approved. You are not asked to pay the membership fee until Thai Immigration has cleared your background check. Payment then goes directly to Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd. — never to us.

How long does the whole process take?

Usually about six to ten weeks from submission to visa. The background approval by Thai Immigration — reviewed across several government agencies — is the longest stage, typically four to eight weeks. Registration and visa stamping take roughly another week.

What documents do I need to start?

To register, you'll need a copy of your passport, the application form, a PDPA consent form, and a recent photograph. We register you the same day we receive them and guide you through each form.

Can my family join?

Yes — the Platinum, Diamond and Reserve tiers offer family add-ons so spouses and dependents can hold their own membership. We'll walk you through the dependent pricing when you choose a tier.

Why apply through Daimaru?

Daimaru has been an authorized General Sales & Services Agent (GSSA) for Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd. since 2015. You speak with the same person from first inquiry through to your visa stamp — no call centers, no handoffs — with support in English and Thai.
