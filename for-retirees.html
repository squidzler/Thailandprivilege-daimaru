For retirees

Retire in Thailand, on your own terms.

A long, settled chapter in Thailand without the yearly visa cycle. The Thailand Privilege Visa gives you 5 to 20 years of residency on a single application — no income proof to apply, no annual bank balance to maintain, and travel in and out whenever you like.

Why it suits retirement

A quieter way to stay.

Many retirees come to the Thailand Privilege Visa after weighing the conventional retirement visa, which typically asks for proof of monthly income or a sizeable deposit held in a Thai bank, renewed every year — and, for some applicants, health insurance. The Privilege route trades that recurring paperwork for a single, longer commitment.

  • No annual renewal run-around. One approval covers the full 5-to-20-year term of your tier, rather than a yearly extension at immigration.
  • No income test and no locked deposit. There is no requirement to prove earnings or to keep a large sum parked in a Thai account.
  • No minimum age. Early retirees qualify just as readily as those past the usual retirement-visa threshold.
  • Come and go freely. Multi-entry travel means visits home, cruises, and trips around the region without re-applying for entry.

It is a long-stay residency visa, not a work permit, and not a path to permanent residence or citizenship — the point is to live in Thailand comfortably, with the admin handled once. See full eligibility and approval details.

Choosing a tier

Which tier fits a retirement?

The right tier depends on how long a horizon you're planning and whether a partner is joining you.

  • Testing the waters — Bronze. Five years, the simplest entry, with all core services. The most-applied tier; its application window runs to 30 September 2026.
  • A longer solo horizon — Diamond. Fifteen years for one person who is settling in for the long term.
  • You and a spouse — Platinum or above. Family eligibility begins at Platinum (ten years), the shortest term that lets a partner join under one membership.
  • The longest commitment — Reserve. Twenty years, by invitation, for those making Thailand a permanent base.

Not sure which? The tier finder recommends one in about a minute, or compare them all on the programs page.

Together

Bringing a partner.

Retirement is rarely a solo decision. From Platinum upward, your membership extends to close family, each screened individually and each issued their own visa.

  • A spouse — including a same-gender spouse — joins as a family member.
  • Your own parents may also be added, if you're supporting an older generation.
  • Parents-in-law are not eligible under the family add-on.

Family add-on rates and rules are set out on the programs page.

In short

Retiree questions, answered plainly.

  • Do I need a large deposit in a Thai bank? No. There is no bank-deposit or income requirement to apply — that's a feature of the conventional retirement visa, not this one.
  • Is there a minimum age? No minimum age applies, so early retirees qualify too.
  • Can I work or run a business? No. It grants residency, not the right to work; a separate work permit would be required for that.
  • Does it cover healthcare or insurance? No. The membership is a visa and concierge service, not health cover — arrange private insurance separately.
