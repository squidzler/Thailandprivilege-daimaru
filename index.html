What it is

A long-term home base in Thailand.

The Thailand Privilege Visa — formerly the Thailand Elite Visa — is a government-backed membership granting multi-entry residency for 5 to 20 years on a single application.

It includes a long-stay visa, airport fast track, a personal liaison, and a 24/7 member center. It does not grant the right to work, and is not a path to citizenship.

A clean background

Residency, not work

The visa grants residency; it does not authorize employment.

Not PR or citizenship

Thailand Privilege is long-term residency, not a route to permanent residency or citizenship.

06 — FAQ

Questions, answered.

How much does the Thailand Privilege Visa cost?

Memberships range from ฿650,000 (~$19,000 USD) for Bronze to ฿5,000,000 (~$146,000 USD) for Reserve. The fee is paid once, directly to Thailand Privilege Card Co., Ltd., and covers the full 5 to 20 year term.

How long does the application take?

Most applications take six to ten weeks from submission to visa. The background check across five Thai government agencies is the longest stage, at four to eight weeks.

Who is eligible?

Any foreign national with a valid passport and a clean background may apply. Approval rests on the security screening rather than income or investment.

Is Thailand Privilege the same as Thailand Elite?

Yes. The Thailand Privilege Visa is the current name for the program formerly called the Thailand Elite Visa, rebranded in October 2023.

What happens if my application is rejected?

You pay nothing until you are approved, so a rejection costs only your time. The membership fee is collected only after Thai authorities clear your application.

Can my family join?

Spouses, including same-gender spouses, plus children, stepchildren, and parents can be added on Platinum, Diamond, and Reserve. Family add-ons run ฿1,000,000 to ฿2,000,000 per member; parents-in-law are not eligible.

Will I owe tax in Thailand?

Thailand taxes residents on certain income, and the rules change over time. Consult a qualified tax advisor about your own situation before relying on any tax assumption.

Can I renew my membership?

Yes. When your term ends you may apply for a new membership at the tiers and rates available at that time.

What is the difference between the tiers?

Tiers differ in length, from 5 to 20 years, and in annual privilege points, from none on Bronze up to 120 on Reserve. Every tier includes the visa, airport fast track, and the 24/7 member center.

Does the visa let me work in Thailand?

No. The Thailand Privilege Visa grants long-term residency, not the right to work, and it is not a path to permanent residency or citizenship.
