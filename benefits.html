Benefits

What being a member actually means.

The visa is the start. Here's what arrives with it — at the airport, in Bangkok, and every year after.

It all comes with the Thailand Privilege Visa (formerly Thailand Elite Visa).

The visa

The visa, plainly.

Multi-entry. The sticker in your passport — the Privilege Entry Visa — is valid for the full duration of your tier: 5, 10, 15, or 20 years.

Each time you land, Thai Immigration stamps a one-year stay permit in your passport. You don't have to leave at any specific point — re-enter whenever, or have us extend your stay annually inside Thailand.

No border runs. No re-entry permits.

At the airport

When you land.

An Elite Personal Assistant (EPA) meets you at the arrival gate the moment you step off the plane. They walk you through the priority Fast Track immigration lane — past the regular queue — help with baggage, and take you to your driver, usually by airport electric cart.

Available at Suvarnabhumi and Phuket international airports, on every arrival and departure. Fast Track lane access (without EPA escort) extends to Don Mueang and Chiang Mai.

EPA = Elite Personal Assistant (airport service). EPL = Elite Personal Liaison (in-Thailand services). Different services, different people.

Inside Thailand

Once you're in.

Once you're a member, daily life in Thailand is straightforward. Most matters you can manage yourself — online or at a local office — with the Member Contact Center on hand whenever you need guidance. And should you prefer everything handled in person, an Elite Personal Liaison can assist, in the Bangkok area.

  • 90-day address report — file it online, drop your passport in person at the Thailand Privilege Card head office, use a paid messenger service, or have a Thailand Privilege partner office handle it if you reside outside Bangkok.
  • Thai bank account — the Member Contact Center arranges your bank reference letter and books the branch appointment.
  • Thai driving license — apply for a Thai license, or convert your existing foreign license, at the Department of Land Transport.
  • Annual stay extension — extend your stay at any local immigration office, wherever is most convenient for you.
  • Visa transfer — when you renew your passport, your visa is transferred to the new one.
Member support

Member Contact Center

Reach the member team in English around the clock, and in Mandarin, Japanese, and Korean from 6am to 9pm (Bangkok time). Your first point of contact for letters, bookings, and questions — anywhere in Thailand.

Optional

Prefer everything handled in person? An EPL can take care of any of the above on your behalf, in the Bangkok area. The first session of each service is complimentary; thereafter, redeem 2 Privilege Points — or ฿3,000 — per service.

Family

For your family.

From Platinum upward, your spouse, your kids (any age — no upper limit), and your own parents can each hold a Privilege visa as additional members. Each gets the full member benefits — their own visa sticker, EPA airport service, member card, Fast Track lane, EPL contact.

Parents-in-law are not eligible.

Tax. Holding the visa does not automatically make you a Thai tax resident. The 180-day rule applies — if you spend 180+ days per calendar year in Thailand, you become a Thai tax resident regardless of which visa you hold. Consult a Thai tax advisor for your specific situation.

Work. Thailand Privilege is a residence visa, not a work permit. Remote work for foreign employers or clients is generally permissible. For paid work inside Thailand, you need a separate work permit, which this program does not provide.
