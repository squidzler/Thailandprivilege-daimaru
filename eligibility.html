Eligibility & Approval

Who qualifies — and why approval is the norm.

The Thailand Privilege Visa (formerly Thailand Elite Visa) is open to almost every foreign national. Eligibility turns on a security background check — not income, not investment — and we confirm where you stand before a single document is filed.

Get a private eligibility check
The short answer

Open to most foreign nationals.

If you hold a passport from a country other than Thailand and have a clean security background, you are very likely eligible. There is no wealth test to clear and no quota to beat.

  • A valid passport from any country except Thailand. The program is designed for foreign nationals seeking long-term residency; Thai citizens do not apply.
  • No income, net-worth, or investment requirement. Unlike investment-based routes elsewhere, the Thailand Privilege Visa asks for no proof of earnings or assets to apply.
  • A clean security background. This is the single substantive requirement, assessed through the government background check described below.
  • Any age may hold a membership. Children and parents can be added from the Platinum tier upward, each screened in their own right.

Not sure where you stand? Ask for a private, no-obligation review — we'll tell you honestly before you commit to anything. Retiring in Thailand? See our guide for retirees. Using Thailand as a travel base? See the guide for frequent travellers.

The one real test

The background check, across five agencies.

Approval rests on a security and financial-integrity screening run by the Thai authorities — not by us, and not by the program operator alone. Once a complete application is submitted, five government bodies review it in parallel. The check typically takes four to eight weeks.

  • Immigration Bureau. Confirms immigration history and standing.
  • Department of Consular Affairs. Verifies passport and identity records.
  • Foreign Affairs Division, Royal Thai Police. Reviews international police records.
  • Central Investigation Bureau. Checks for criminal history relevant to entry.
  • Anti-Money Laundering Office (AMLO). Screens for financial-integrity concerns.

End to end — preparation, screening, registration, and visa affixation — most applicants are members within six to ten weeks. See the full step-by-step process.

Worth raising early

What can stand in the way.

A small number of factors materially affect approval. Raising them with us at the start costs you nothing and protects you from a declined application later — everything you share is reviewed privately.

  • A criminal record or open proceedings. A serious criminal history, or active criminal proceedings, can prevent approval. Minor or long-resolved matters are best discussed candidly so we can assess them.
  • A prior Thai Volunteer visa. A previous Volunteer visa is a known complication and must be disclosed up front.
  • Improper use of an ED (education) visa. An education visa used for something other than genuine study can affect eligibility.
  • Incomplete or inconsistent documents. The most common — and the most fixable. We check every document for completeness and consistency before anything is filed.

None of these is an automatic refusal in every case. The honest answer depends on the specifics, which is exactly what a pre-qualification review is for.

Bringing your family

Who can join your membership.

From the Platinum tier upward, your membership can extend to close family. Each person receives their own visa and is screened individually.

  • Your spouse — including a same-gender spouse.
  • Your children and stepchildren — of any age.
  • Your parents.
  • Parents-in-law are not eligible under the family add-on.

Family add-ons are available on Platinum, Diamond, and Reserve. Compare the tiers on the Programs page, or use the tier finder to see which fits a family plan.

What the visa is — and isn't

Residency, not a work permit.

  • Long-stay, multi-entry residency. You can live in Thailand and travel in and out freely for the full term of your tier, from 5 to 20 years.
  • Not the right to work. The visa does not authorize employment in Thailand; a separate work permit would be required for that.
  • Not a path to permanent residence or citizenship. It is a long-term privilege visa, renewed by its membership term rather than converting to PR.
  • Renewable stay stamps for continuous residents. Members living in Thailand continuously coordinate the in-passport stay stamp through immigration; the membership itself is paid once and needs no annual fee.

For tax questions tied to long-term residency, consult a qualified tax advisor — we do not provide tax or legal advice.

In short

Eligibility, answered plainly.

  • Is there an income requirement? No. There is no income, net-worth, or investment test to apply. The membership fee is paid once, and only after approval.
  • What is the single real requirement? Passing the Thai government background check — a security and financial-integrity screening across five agencies.
  • Can I work on this visa? No. It grants long-stay residency, not the right to work, and it is not a path to permanent residence or citizenship.
