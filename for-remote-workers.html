First, the honest part

Residency to live, not a permit to work.

This deserves to come before the perks, because it is where remote workers most often get the wrong idea. The Thailand Privilege Visa lets you live in Thailand for the long term — it does not, by itself, grant the right to work here.

  • Working online for clients or an employer abroad is how many members use the visa, but how that sits for tax and immigration depends on your own situation.
  • Employment with a Thai company is a separate matter and requires a work permit, which the membership does not provide.
  • We don't give tax or legal advice. Before you rely on any arrangement, speak with a qualified professional who knows your circumstances.

With that clear, the appeal for location-independent workers is mostly about stability — which is the rest of this page. See full eligibility and approval details.

Why it suits the work

A base you can actually build on.

Living somewhere is different from passing through it. A multi-year visa is what lets you stop improvising and put the foundations of a working life in place.

  • Sign a real lease. A long horizon makes a proper apartment — not a month-to-month rental at tourist rates — a sensible commitment.
  • Open the door to local banking and services. Long-stay residency makes the everyday admin of living here far more straightforward than a short stamp allows.
  • Settle into a routine, not a checkout date. A dedicated desk, a gym, a community — the things that make remote work sustainable rather than nomadic.
  • Stop the visa maths. One approval covers the full term of your tier, so the tourist-entry cycle and the border runs simply end.

Choosing a tier

Which tier matches your horizon?

For a working base, the deciding questions are how long you expect to stay and whether a partner is moving with you.

  • Trying it for a while — Bronze. Five years, the simplest entry, with airport fast track and all core services. The most-applied tier; its application window runs to 30 September 2026.
  • Settling in to build — Diamond. Fifteen years for one person making Thailand the base for a business or career chapter.
  • Moving with a partner — Platinum or above. Family eligibility starts at Platinum (ten years), the shortest term that lets a spouse join under one membership.
  • The long game — Reserve. Twenty years, by invitation, for those treating Thailand as a permanent home base.

Not sure which? The tier finder suggests one in about a minute, or compare them all on the programs page.

In short

Remote-work questions, answered plainly.

  • Can I work remotely on this visa? It grants residency, not the right to work locally. Working online for overseas clients is a personal tax and immigration question — take advice.
  • Is it the same as the Destination Thailand Visa? No — they're separate routes. This is a 5-to-20-year membership; we'll say honestly if another visa fits you better.
  • Will I still do visa runs? No. The fixed-term membership replaces tourist-entry stays entirely.
  • Does it include health or device insurance? No. It's a visa and concierge service, not insurance — arrange your own cover.
