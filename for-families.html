For families

Move as a family, under one membership.

Relocating with a partner and children turns a personal decision into a household one. From the Platinum tier upward, the Thailand Privilege Visa lets you add close family to a single membership — each person screened in their own right and issued their own long-stay visa, all on the same term.

Find the tier that fits your family
Who can join

Family, clearly defined.

The program is specific about who counts as family, so it helps to know the lines before you plan. Family add-ons are available on the Platinum, Diamond, and Reserve tiers.

  • A spouse — including a same-gender spouse — joins as a family member with their own visa.
  • Children and stepchildren can be added, so the whole household moves together on one membership.
  • Your own parents are eligible, if an older generation is coming with you.
  • Parents-in-law are not eligible under the family add-on — a common point worth knowing early.

Each added member is screened individually, exactly as the main applicant is — see eligibility and approval for how that works.

Why it suits a household

Stability for the whole table.

  • One long term for everyone. The family shares a single multi-year residency, rather than juggling separate visas with separate renewal dates.
  • No annual renewal cycle. A single approval covers the full term of the tier — fewer immigration appointments around school terms and work.
  • Travel together, freely. Multi-entry travel suits family trips home and around the region without re-applying to return.
  • Help that covers the household. Airport fast track and the member contact centre extend to the family, not just the main member.
Choosing a tier

Which tier fits a family?

Because family members can only be added from Platinum upward, the choice for households starts there and rises with the horizon you're planning.

  • The family entry point — Platinum. Ten years, the shortest term that allows family add-ons, with family-friendly services included.
  • A longer chapter — Diamond. Fifteen years, suited to families settling in for schooling and beyond.
  • The longest commitment — Reserve. Twenty years, by invitation, for families making Thailand a permanent home.
  • A single member testing first — Bronze or Gold. One adult can start solo on a shorter tier; note that family add-ons begin only at Platinum.

Compare terms and family add-on rates on the programs page, or let the tier finder narrow it down.

Worth knowing

What it does, and doesn't, cover.

  • It's residency, not a work permit. Adults can live in Thailand on the visa; taking local employment requires a separate work permit.
  • Schooling is arranged separately. Children can reside on the visa, but enrolment is handled directly with the school of your choice — the membership is not tied to it.
  • Insurance is your own. The membership is a visa and concierge service, not health cover — arrange family insurance separately.
  • On tax: moving a household abroad raises tax questions. We don't advise on tax — consult a qualified advisor.
On cost

The main fee, plus each member.
